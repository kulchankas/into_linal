Start here — the foundations

Do this once, up front: it makes every chapter below far easier.

Watch & keep open
  • 📺 3Blue1Brown — Essence of Linear Algebra (≈3 h total). The geometric intuition for vectors, span, transformations, determinant, eigenvectors. Watch the whole series first.
  • 📖 Mathematics for Machine Learning (Deisenroth, Faisal & Ong — free PDF). Part I, chapters 2–4 are literally linear algebra for data science; keep it as your DS-facing companion.
  • 🎓 MIT 18.06 — Linear Algebra (Gilbert Strang) — the classic video course; lectures are mapped per chapter below.
  • 📐 Axler — Linear Algebra Done Right (free online). The abstract, proof-first take — best match for this course's dual-space / inner-product flavour.

Self-study plan, chapter by chapter

Ch 1 Vector Spacesspaces & subspaces · basis & direct sum · linear maps · dual space · matrix representation · projections & symmetries · metric & normed spaces · operator norm
📺 Watch
3B1B Essence ch. 1–4, 7–8, 13 (vectors → linear combinations/span/basis → linear transformations → matrix multiplication → inverse, column space, null space → change of basis).
📖 Read
MML ch. 2 (Linear Algebra); Axler ch. 1–3 (vector spaces, finite-dimensional spaces, linear maps & the dual); Strang 18.06 lec. 1–3, 5–10 (spaces, subspaces, the four fundamental subspaces). Course textbook ch. 1.
🎯 Course exercises
1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, 1.22, 1.23, 1.24, 1.28, 1.29, 1.31, 1.33, 1.37, 1.38, 1.40, 1.42, 1.43, 1.44, 1.45.
🤖 In data science
Feature spaces and embeddings are vector spaces; linear models are linear maps; norms drive regularization ($L^1$/$L^2$); the operator norm bounds a layer's stability/Lipschitz constant.
Ch 2 Inner-Product Spacesinner products · orthonormal bases & Gram–Schmidt · orthogonal projection · least-squares / supervised learning · Riesz theorem · adjoint operators
📺 Watch
3B1B Essence ch. 9 (dot products & duality) — and revisit ch. 13 (change of basis) for orthonormal coordinates.
📖 Read
MML ch. 3 (Analytic Geometry — inner products, projections, orthogonality); Axler ch. 6 (inner-product spaces); Strang 18.06 lec. 14–17 (orthogonality, projections, least squares, Gram–Schmidt). Course textbook ch. 2.
🎯 Course exercises
2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.4, 2.7, 2.8, 2.11, 2.12, 2.13, 2.15, 2.17, 2.18 (programming).
🤖 In data science
Least-squares regression is orthogonal projection onto the column space; Gram–Schmidt → QR; orthonormal bases underpin PCA; inner products generalize to kernels (the kernel trick).
Ch 3 Sesquilinear & Bilinear Formsbilinear/sesquilinear forms · matrix representation · tensor product
📖 Read
Course textbook ch. 3 is the main source (this topic is light in video courses). Support: MML ch. 3 (bilinear forms / metrics) and Axler ch. 9 for the form viewpoint; for tensor products, read the textbook's section carefully and cross-check with MML's notation.
🎯 Course exercises
3.1, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 3.6, 3.8, 3.9, 3.10, 3.17.
🤖 In data science
Bilinear/quadratic forms = covariance matrices and Mahalanobis distance; tensor products model multilinear data and power tensor decompositions and the bilinear scores inside attention.
Ch 4 Determinantsdefinition & properties · alternating $n$-linear forms · determinant & volume · Cramer's rule
📺 Watch
3B1B Essence ch. 6 (the determinant — as a signed area/volume scaling factor).
📖 Read
Strang 18.06 lec. 18–20 (determinants, formulas, applications); Axler ch. 10 (determinants from alternating forms — matches this course); MML ch. 4.1. Course textbook ch. 4.
🎯 Course exercises
4.1, 4.5, 4.7, 4.9, 4.13.
🤖 In data science
The Jacobian determinant is the change-of-variables factor in normalizing flows; $\log\det$ appears in Gaussian likelihoods; determinant = volume scaling under a linear map.
Ch 5 Eigenvalues & Spectral Theoryeigenvalues/-vectors/-spaces · characteristic polynomial · diagonalization · Cayley–Hamilton · matrix functions · triangularization · spectral theorems · SVD
📺 Watch
3B1B Essence ch. 14 (eigenvectors & eigenvalues) and ch. 15 (abstract vector spaces); plus 3B1B's SVD / PCA explainers.
📖 Read
MML ch. 4 (Matrix Decompositions — eigendecomposition & SVD, the DS-critical chapter); Strang 18.06 lec. 21–22, 25, 29–30 (eigenvalues, symmetric matrices, SVD); Axler ch. 5, 7 (eigenvalues, the spectral theorem). Course textbook ch. 5.
🎯 Course exercises
5.1, 5.2, 5.3, 5.5, 5.6, 5.8, 5.10, 5.14, 5.15, 5.16, 5.17, 5.19, 5.22, 5.25, 5.29, 5.31.
🤖 In data science
The headline chapter: PCA = eigendecomposition of the covariance; SVD powers dimensionality reduction, recommender systems and latent-semantic analysis; spectral clustering and PageRank are eigenproblems; matrix functions (e.g. $e^{tA}$) model graph diffusion and dynamics.

How to use this
  • Each week: watch that chapter's 📺 clips → skim the 📖 reading → do the week's 🎯 exercises → only then read the textbook proofs in full.
  • For depth: Axler for proofs, Strang for computation, MML for the data-science framing — pick by what you're stuck on.
  • Programming (Ex. 2.18): use NumPy/Julia; verify least-squares against numpy.linalg.lstsq and decompositions against numpy.linalg.svd.
